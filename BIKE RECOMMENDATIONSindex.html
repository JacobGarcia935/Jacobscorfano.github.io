What is a "Full Suspension" and what is a "HardTail"

A full-suspension mountain bike is a type of bike that has suspension (shock absorbers) both in the front (fork) and rear (rear shock) of the bike. This suspension system helps to absorb bumps and provide a smoother ride, especially on rough terrain. It gives you better traction and control, making it suitable for challenging trails and especially those jumps. On the other hand, a hardtail mountain bike has suspension only in the front (fork) of the bike, while the rear remains rigid or "hard." This type of bike is generally lighter and more efficient when it comes to pedaling. Hardtails are commonly used for less extreme trails and smoother surfaces, as they provide good efficiency and control while still offering some comfort through the front suspension. In summary, a full suspension bike has suspension in both the front and rear, providing better control and comfort on rough trails and jumps. A hardtail bike, on the other hand, has suspension only in the front, offering a lighter and more efficient ride
